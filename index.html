Dixieland

Sometimes referred to as Classic Jazz or Traditional Jazz, Dixieland is the original form of jazz developed in New Orleans in the early 1900s. Dixieland combines elements of African American/New Orleans ragtime as well as Sicilian music, and often features the trumpet playing the melody while other intruments improvise around it. Dixieland was the style of such greats as Louis Armstrong, King Oliver, and The Original Dixieland Jazz Band.

Original Dixieland Jazz Band - Tiger Rag

Louis Armstrong - When the Saints go Marching In

Swing

Swing was the music of the 1930s and 40s. Named after its distinct 'swing feel,' this style of jazz requires listeners to move to the music, inspiring such dances as the Jitterbug and the Lindy Hop. This era was spearheaded by big band leaders such as Benny Goodman, Duke Ellignton, and Glenn Miller.

Benny Goodman - Sing, Sing, Sing

Glenn Miller - In the Mood

Bebop

Bebop emerged from Swing as a new generation of jazz musicians began experimenting with faster tempos and more complex chord progressions. Where Swing wants you to get up and dance, Bop wants you to sit and listen ot the musical genious of the performers. Bebop groups tend to be much smaller and focus more on improvisation than heavily arranged tunes. Some of the pioneers of this style include Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie, Thelonious Monk, and Art Blakey.

Dizzy Gillespie - Night in Tunisia

Art Blakey - Moanin'

Cool Jazz

In contrast to Bebop, Cool Jazz offeres a mellower feel that features long melodic lines and occasional classical elements. Epitomized by Miles Davis' Birth of the Cool, Cool Jazz offers the technicality of Bebop but in a lighter and more relaxed fashion. Some of the cool jazz greats include Chet Baker, Dave Brubeck, Gil Evans, and early Miles Davis.

Miles Davis - Boplicity

Chet Baker - Almost Blue

Free Jazz

Also known as Avante-Garde Jazz, Free Jazz came into prominence in the late 1950s and early 60s as musicians began to tire of the fixed structure of Bebop. Free Jazz is characterized by its dissmissal of traditional jazz framework, such as the twelve-bar blues, in favor of more improvisation. Pioneers of this genre include Pharoah Sanders, the great John Coltrane, Charles Mingus, and Eric Dolphy.

John Coltrane - Ascension

Charles Mingus - Pithecanthropus Erectus

Jazz Fusion

Jazz Fusion, also known as "jazz rock" takes the classic elements of jazz and mixes them with electric intruments suchs as electric guitars and keyboards. Fusion came to prominence in the late 1960s with innovations from the likes of Miles Davis, who on his seminal fusion album Bitches Brew, began incorporating more electronic elements and even played his trumpet though electronic effect pedals. This style was a favorite of Herbie Hancock, Weather Report, and Miles Davis in his later years.

Miles Davis - Bitches Brew

Weather Report - Teen Town

Latin Jazz

Latin Jazz is comprised of two main styles Afro-Cuban Jazz and Bossa Nova. The former is denoted by its Cuban dance inspired use of claves, congas, and bongos; and was popularized in the 1940s by Mario Bauza and Machito. The latter draws from Brazilian samba culture and is epitomized in Stan Getz and Joao Gilberto's The Girl from Impanema.

Mario Bauza & His Afro-Cuban Jazz Orchestra - Asi No, Papa

Stan Getz/Joao Gilberto - The Girl From Impanema

Smooth Jazz

Arising in the late 1970s and early 1980s, this easy listening style of jazz combines elements of fusion with popular music. Lead by musicians such as Kenny G, Grover Washington, and Chuck Mangione, smooth jazz often recevies criticism for not being as technical as other forms of jazz. Despite this, smooth jazz was a huge commercial success, with Kenny G becoming one of the best-selling musicians of all time.

Grover Washington Jr. - Mister Magic

Kenny G - Songbird

Acid Jazz

This style of jazz was developed in the London club scene during the 1980s. Acid Jazz takes elements from soul, funk, and disco and often features sampling. With an emphasis on the percussion, the main goal of Acid Jazz, like Swing, is to get audiences dancing. Some of the biggest bands to embrace this genre include Jamiroquai, Us3, and A Tribe Called Quest.

Jamiroquai - Virtual Insanity

Us3 - Cantaloop (Flip Fantasia)
